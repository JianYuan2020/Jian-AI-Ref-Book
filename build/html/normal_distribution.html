Normal Distribution¶

A Normal (or Gaussian or Gauss or Laplace-Gauss) distribution, Notation \({\mathcal {N}}(\mu ,\sigma ^{2})\), is a type of continuous probability distribution for a real-valued random variable. The general form of its probability density function is

\({\displaystyle f(x)={\frac {1}{\sigma {\sqrt {2\pi }}}}e^{-{\frac {1}{2}}\left({\frac {x-\mu }{\sigma }}\right)^{2}}}\)

Mean¶

\(\mu = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} x_{i}\)

Variance¶

\(\sigma^{2} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_{i} - \mu)^2\)

Note: here we use \(\frac{1}{N}\) instead of \(\frac{1}{N - 1}\)

Standard Deviation¶

\(\sigma = \sqrt {\sigma^{2}} = \sqrt {\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_{i} - \mu)^2}\)

Here \({\displaystyle x_{i}, \mu, \sigma^{2}, \sigma \in \mathbb {R} }\) and \(x\) = { \(x_{1}, x_{2}, ..., x_{i}, ..., x_{N}\) }.
