Gaussian (Normal) Distribution¶

Let’s assume:

  • Training set: \(X = \{ x^{(1)}, x^{(2)}, ..., x^{(i)}, ..., x^{(m)} \}\) and \({\displaystyle x^{(i)} \in \mathbb {R^{n}}}\)

  • All features are distributed as Normal Distribution

  • That is: \(x_{1}\) ~ \({\mathcal {N}}(\mu_{1}, \sigma _{1}^{2})\), \(x_{2}\) ~ \({\mathcal {N}}(\mu_{2}, \sigma _{2}^{2})\), …, \(x_{n}\) ~ \({\mathcal {N}}(\mu_{n}, \sigma _{n}^{2})\)

Then:

  • \(p(x) = p(x_{1}; \mu_{1}, \sigma _{1}^{2})\) * \(p(x_{2}; \mu_{2}, \sigma _{2}^{2})\) * … * \(p(x_{n}; \mu_{n}, \sigma _{n}^{2})\)

  • \(p(x) = \Pi_{j=1}^{n} p(x_{j}; \mu_{j}, \sigma _{j}^{2})\)
